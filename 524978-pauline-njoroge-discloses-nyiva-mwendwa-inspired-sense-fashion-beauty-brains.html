Pauline Njoroge Discloses Nyiva Mwendwa Inspired Her Sense of Fashion: &quot;Beauty With Brains&quot;

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  680 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • Winnie Nyiva Mwendwa was the first woman ever to be appointed as a Cabinet minister in Kenya since independence
  • Besides her political ascendance to admirable levels, her sense of fashion remains a topic that cannot be left out when talking about Nyiva
  • Her boldness, brilliance, and tasteful sense of fashion are some of the secrets behind the meteoric rise of Jubilee Party Deputy Organising Secretary Pauline Njoroge

Nyiva Mwendwa is a trailblazing woman in Kenya's professional and political sphere, having been appointed as the first woman minister during the tenure of former president, the late Daniel Moi.

Who is Nyiva Mwendwa?

Mwendwa was appointed Minister for Culture and Social Services on May 9, 1995, a whopping 32 years since independence, a fete that former president Uhuru Kenyatta noted during the 2022 Mashujaa Day celebrations.

The celebrated trailblazer and alumnae of Alliance Girls High School began as a teacher before ascending to become the country's first female minister in an era where patriarchy was the norm.

Besides her excellence, the graduate of Manchester University cannot be described without mentioning her sense of fashion and self-love, as most millennials would put it.

She is infamously remembered for allegedly carrying her hairdresser to the Beijing World Conference of Women in 1995, putting her in a tight spot as criticism and harsh words flew from one corner to another towards her.

Nyiva Mwendwa, hairdresser to Beijing saga

In an interview with The Standard, she dismissed the allegations, saying that she had led a delegation of 450 women from all professional categories, and a hairdresser happened to be part of the group.

As criticism hit Nyiva and her counterparts, a young girl with big dreams to ascend to the helm of greatness in Kenya was admiring her sense of fashion, assertiveness, and excellence.

Pauline Njoroge age

Pauline Njoroge, 38, who was nominated to the Jubilee Party National Executive Council (NEC) as the deputy organising secretary by former president Uhuru Kenyatta, was watching closely what women were doing in Beijing from a little TV set in her home.

In a Facebook post with an elegant photo of herself in a body-hugging long dress, Njoroge reminisced how she developed a passion for the excellent sense of fashion and attributed part to Mwendwa.

"I have always loved dressing up. When I was a kid, I would wear my mom’s dresses and tie them in a way I thought looked fashionable. I wanted to look like Esther Muoso of KBC or Nyiva Mwendwa, the minister. "At the age of 10, I remember being glued to the TV watching the recaps of the Beijing Women’s Conference of 1995. My main attraction was how the participants were dressed. It was said that Nyiva Mwendwa had carried her hairdresser to the conference, and I found that intriguing. It’s a thing I would do," narrated Njoroge.

Pauline Njoroge opens up on her elegant sense of fashion inspiration.

The beautiful and brilliant politician with a passion for youth confessed that she still had the little girl who loved dresses and admired powerful women with an irresistible sense of fashion inside of her.

"I always aspire to emulate them because they set the pace and showed us that a woman can be beautiful, brainy, and elegant/fashionable. Beauty with a brain should not be an exception but a norm because this world is full of beautiful women doing great exploits in various fields," Njoroge added.

True to her dream, Njoroge has kept ascending the political and leadership ladder coupled with her dignified sense of fashion.

As tender and gentle as she may appear in her delightful photos, Njoroge is as fierce as she is brilliant in politics and is criticised as she is celebrated by her foes and allies, respectively.
