Che Hongcai, 78, who is now retired, set out to compile the dictionary in 1978 while he was a professor at the Beijing-based Communication University of China. He secured a deal with The Commercial Press, one of the mainland’s oldest publishers. The dictionary, which contains 50,000 entries and 2.5 million characters, is finally going to print this year and, according to his contract, Che will be paid 80 yuan (HK$101) for every 1,000 characters – amounting to 200,000 yuan.
