Call centre worker who made 76 a month quits for OnlyFans and now earns fortune

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  547 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

A former call centre worker has opened up about how she went from making £76 a month to being able to support her whole family after finding fame on OnlyFans.

CJ Miles grew up in difficult circumstances in the Philippines, with her parents leaving her homeless at the age of only 13.

This meant the model was forced to sofa-surf between school classmates’ houses, and she decided to head to university to find her way out of poverty.

And, after a complicated journey that found the 4ft 7in bombshell meeting one of the original founders of OnlyFans, CJ eventually made her fortune by working as an adult model.

Sharing her story on Holly Randall’s Unfiltered podcast, CJ began by explaining her computer engineering qualifications.

She noted: “I know how to make websites with programming languages."

But, despite her academic ability, CJ said that she initially found work hard to come by while living in the Philippines, and ended up working in a low-paid role.

“I worked in a call centre… as a tech support. So when you call the 1-800 number when you have a problem with computers, it’s always routed straight to India or the Philippines because the cost of labour is cheaper,” she explained.

“I wanted to leave the Philippines because I was only making $100 (roughly £76) a month… I lived in a dorm with 12 girls in bunk beds for 8 years.”

At the time, CJ was dating her American boss, and the pair eventually decided to elope, enabling CJ to move to the States.

However, the couple’s relationship quickly deteriorated.

The former call centre employee ended up finding work in the famous Spearmint Rhino Las Vegas strip club after being noticed for sharing photos on MySpace.

This decision was the start of a lucrative career in the adult industry, as she began sharing content on subscription site OnlyFans.

In fact, CJ claimed that she had been friendly with the first owner of the platform Bill Fox, who she said had followed her on an alternative site.

She recalled: “I met him at his house, and he showed me all the girls and guys on OnlyFans that make money, and was like, ‘you have to start your own’.

“I quit dancing – because I was dancing for 12 years – and I moved to Miami, and I was like, ‘no, I’m not going to dance any more, I’m going to focus on OnlyFans’.

“Now I have assistants helping me, because after Covid it became big.”

And, having gained over 2.2 million followers on Instagram, CJ has found huge success sharing explicit images and videos.

“I support my whole family, my brother, sister and all my nephews to finish college because of what I do,” she said.
