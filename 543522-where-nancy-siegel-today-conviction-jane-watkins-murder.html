Who is Nancy Siegel?

She was a Baltimore resident, born Nancy Sweitzer in 1948. Her father raised her when her mother ran out of the family. Unfortunately, her father was killed in 1964 outside a bar.

During her teenage years, Nancy danced on The Buddy Deane Show, a local dance show that inspired American Bandstand and the musical Hairspray.

The story of Nancy Siegel

Nancy engaged in a several decades-long fraud scheme. She lured her victims into trusting her before obtaining their personal financial information, mostly through romantic connections.

Nancy and her close family members

Nancy tied the knot with her first husband, Charles Kucharksi, in 1968. Shortly after their marriage, she started staking money and taking out credit in his name behind his back. By the time they divorced in 1985, she had over $100,000 in debt.

Nancy Siegel's daughters were also their mother's victims. She used their identities to create credit accounts in their names without their consent. She directed both statements to the addresses she controlled.

By the spring of 1999, Nancy had started dating another man, Eric Siegel, who eventually became her third husband.

In September 1993, his mail started being forwarded to Nancy’s residence without his permission or knowledge. They broke up in the fall of 1994, but there are no details about how much she took from him.

What happened to Mr Watkins?

He was murdered within 48 hours of 13 May 1996 after Nancy's drive to Virginia. She dumped his body close to some trash on an access point to the Appalachian Trail in Loudoun County.

According to the FBI records, the investigators located Mr Watkin’s emaciated body almost immediately, and the autopsy found the following:

  • Fresh cuts and abrasions on the body
  • A blunt force trauma to the back of his head
  • Toxic levels of a sedative in his body

Unfortunately, since there was no identification with his remains, Mr Watkin’s body was not identified until January 2003, when the authorities used military records.

Federal prosecutors alleged that after Watkins' death, Nancy stole $89,728 in Social Security payments and around $10,000 of his retirement annuity.

What was Nancy Siegel’s sentence?

According to Case Text, the Baltimore resident was sentenced to 400 months of imprisonment followed by five years of supervised release on 23 April 2009. She was imprisoned for the following counts:

  • One count of witness tampering murder
  • Seven counts of theft of government property
  • Six counts of bank fraud
  • Two counts of fraudulent use of identification means
  • Two counts of mail fraud
  • Two counts of wire fraud

After the sentencing, United States Attorney Rod J Rosenstein released a statement that in part read:

Nancy persuaded Jack Watkins and stole his money, then she murdered him, put his naked body in a trunk and dumped it in the trash so that she could continue to collect his Social Security and retirement benefits for seven years. Today’s sentence of 400 months in prison ensures that Nancy Siegel’s horrendous life of con games and murder is over for good.

Where is Nancy Siegel today?

Nancy is still serving her sentence at the Federal Correctional Institution-Hazelton in Bruceton Mills, West Virginia.

She is scheduled to be released on 7 January 2032. However, after serving close to 18 years of her jail time, Nancy sought a reduction of her sentence under the "compassionate release" statutory provision but was denied.
