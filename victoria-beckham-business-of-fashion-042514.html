Victoria Beckham Talks Making it in Fashion

 Posted on February 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  460 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Media Platforms Design Team

- "The most valuable part of being famous is that you have a voice and people will listen...But I don't like to use that card very often. I like to keep my head down, work hard, focus on what I'm doing business-wise. I like to try and control how much I'm seen. I don't want to go out and be photographed every day, you know." Victoria Beckham discusses her early days in fashion and how she made it in the industry with Imran Amed for the cover story of Business of Fashion's second print edition. Remembering when she first started, Beckham recalls, "For a long time there, I was a bit of a laughingstock. And while everybody was busy laughing, what was I doing? I was laying the foundation to what I have in place now." Business of Fashion

- Say it ain't so, Solange! The singer revealed to ASOS magazine that she has "a bit of print fatigue." Refinery29

- Two robberies rocked Dior and Céline's Paris shops on Wednesday, with thieves getting away with nearly 100,000 euros. Fashion United

- Bare Escentuals enters the realm of liquid makeup with a new line of foundation, bareSkin, launching next Thursday. WWD

- Gwyneth Paltrow, Cara Delevingne, Adele, Jerry Hall, and Georgia May Jagger are just a few of the famous names who customized a Fendi Peekaboo bag to celebrate the opening of the fashion house's Bond Street store in London. The bags will be auctioned in honor of the children's charity Kids Company. Telegraph

- Reese Witherspoon's new lifestyle company will be called Draper James, with its first store opening next year. E!

- Lady Gaga models Versace's StudsLadies in the house's new eyewear campaign. Versace

- Intermix honored BAZAAR's own Laura Brown at the Gramercy Park Hotel on Tuesday. Intermix

- Join Madewell and Vans for the launch of their collaboration next Tuesday, April 29, from 6-8pm at Madewell's Soho location in NYC (486 Broadway). Madewell

- Neiman Marcus and Bergdorf Goodman have joined with the Fashion Project to launch the White Glove donation service. Join the stores' InCircle loyalty program and a Fashion Project stylist will pick up your gently-used clothing and accessories to donate to a charity of your choosing. Members will receive $100 gift cards to Neiman Marcus or Bergdorf's for every 10 items donated. Fashion Project
