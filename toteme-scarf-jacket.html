Why the Totme Scarf Coat Went Viral

 Posted on July 18, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  751 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Where were you when you first laid eyes on Totême's scarf jacket? Was it during a commute in New York or London? Was it scrolling TikTok with one of 208,000-plus other users who've all watched videos tagged with the coat? Or, like me, was it at Fashion Week last February, when every other showgoer was seen wearing it?

Regardless of how your first fateful encounter happened, there's growing consensus online that Totême's oversized scarf jacket is a moment in outerwear. The moment, even.

Totême's Stockholm-based founders weren't available for comment to explain how their must-have coat, first released for pre-fall 2022, came to be, or why they think it's causing a frenzy online now. I can venture a few guesses, however, after speaking to women who already own it.

On the one hand, as stylist and personal shopper Imogen Alexander tells me, "it's practical perfection." It's 88 percent wool (so, it's warm despite the drapey blanket fit), and there's zero risk of forgetting your winter scarf when it's sewn into your jacket.

"The in-between-seasons coat is so hard to find, and this one ticks all the necessary boxes," agrees stylist and Favorite Daughter style director Jordan Foster, who owns the gray version. "The coat itself is completely unique from the contrast stitch details to the attached scarf."

Simply saying the coat's appeal lies in its practicality would fail to capture its magic. There are, after all, hundreds of perfectly practical and oversized wool coats churned out each season. Not every one of them inspires the "NEED THIS" comments like this one.

"Part of the reason behind the scarf coat's success, I think, is that it looks old-school without looking vintage, so it feels classic in this very fresh way," BAZAAR fashion news director Rachel Tashjian muses. A fan of "Totême's specific sense of freaky minimalism," she wore a puffer version of the scarf jacket to her wedding weekend last month. "It looks a zillion times more expensive than it is. And the padded/puffer version also looks very old-school, too, but with this little Tabasco dash of eccentricity, so it's almost Charles James-y or Schiaparelli-ish."

Fashion newsletter Magasin's founder and editor, Laura Reilly, tells me the scarf jacket crossed her radar around its initial release, then again in late summer when it was submitted to her in a fall wish list reader poll. Finally, Reilly was convinced to track down the black-stitched version on Poshmark a few weeks ago after a scarf jacket sighting at the Williamsburg, Brooklyn, restaurant Marlowe & Sons.

"I think it takes time for things to percolate, but it was one of those dot-connecting moments where all of the comforts I felt were lacking in my outerwear wardrobe were solved by this one item," she says in an email. "I ordered one the next day or maybe the following, whenever it was, it was an urgent feeling."

Reilly notes that the coat's slow-burn rise is following a course charted by It jackets and coats of seasons past. "The Nanuskha vegan puffer, the Saks Potts Foxy coat, and the Max Mara teddy all started in the ultra-niche and only hit their stride after awareness spread over the course of a full season. By the third year, peak saturation will have been reached, and too many knockoffs will kill the hype, but for now, the Totême coat is past its 'educational' phase and is enjoying its prime," she explains.

Shopping in the "prime" era hasn't been easy. The coat is out of stock in several sizes both at Totême's online storefront and partner retailers like Mytheresa and Net-a-Porter. But the coat could still soon be yours: A Totême rep tells BAZAAR the label is restocking both sold-out colors (gray and black) in December. You can also scan consignment shops for a potential straggler.

Whether you can secure the jacket or not, know that your interest is at least a sign of taste. "I almost don't want you to write about it," one editor told me when I'd first suggested writing about the coat, "because I really want it for myself."
