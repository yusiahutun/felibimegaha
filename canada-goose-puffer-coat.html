3 ways to style a puffer coat for the new season

 Posted on July 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  496 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Fashion and practicality rarely go hand in hand, but there are occasions where the two combust with a perfect synergy. The puffer jacket – long beloved by outdoor activists and fashion types with equal fervour – is one example that’s enjoyed a particular favour among the fashion crowd in the last six months.

Kylie Jenner started wearing vintage John Galliano-for-Dior puffers during ski trips to Aspen, Hailey Bieber’s emerald green puffer vest became an overnight social-media sensation, and brands spanning Schiaparelli, Louis Vuitton and Miu Miu are all embracing the look on the runway. We are, it seems, in the midst of a full-blown puffer renaissance.

Looking to incorporate the look into your own wardrobe? You’d be remiss not to reach for Canada Goose, the long-time favourite puffer jackets of the in-the-know fashion obsessives. The brand began in 1957 as purveyors of down jackets and other practical outerwear, quickly developing a cult following, thanks to the clothing’s performance luxury design (some designs are tested to withstand temperatures of up to -25°C) and understated good looks.

As the brand launches its latest collection, Pastels, inspired by the soft hues of the natural landscape, BAZAAR road-tests four new designs, showcasing how to incorporate them into your everyday wardrobe.

1. The weekend coffee run

Canada Goose Everett Vest in 'North Star White', £495, CANADA GOOSE; Tôteme baseball cap, £200 at FARFETCH; Canada Goose Muskoka Hoody in 'Moonstone Grey', £350 at CANADA GOOSE; New Balance trainers, £180 at MATCHESFASHION.COM; Balenciaga tote, £1,250 at MYTHERESA; Khaite scarf, £650 at NET-A-PORTER

Looking presentable while still wearing your favourite tracksuit is one of life’s great riddles. The answer, however, is refreshingly simple: just add a luxe puffer vest. Canada Goose’s off-white creation is fleece-lined and impossibly warm. Finish the look off with a rotation of polished accessories and Saturday-morning walks around the park never looked so good.

2. The Sunday brunch

Simone Rocha earrings, £195 at FARFETCH; Chloé jeans, £565 at MATCHESFASHION.COM; Loro Piana turtleneck, £790 at HARRODS; Canada Goose Junction Parka in 'Lucent Rose', £950, CANADA GOOSE; Saint Laurent loafers, £565 at NET-A-PORTER; Gucci handbag, £660 at MYTHERESA

The perfect rosé-filled Sunday brunch look is formulaic, but it works every time: a pair of cropped flare jeans (Chloé's neo-1970s vibe always looks modern) paired with chunky loafers and a refined cashmere knit. Finish it off with an oversized down jacket in an unexpected hue and you’re golden.

3. The after-work drinks

Canada Goose Byward Parka in 'Meltwater', £995, CANADA GOOSE; Paco Rabanne dress, £670 at NET-A-PORTER; Otiumberg necklace, £220 at MATCHESFASHION.COM; Ayedē boots, £550 at MYTHERESA; Saint Laurent handbag, £1,890 at BROWNS

Don’t shelve your favourite floral printed knee-length silk dress from autumn 'til spring. Instead, swap out your favourite sandals for a pair of chunky knee-high boots, then throw on this quilted Canada Goose coat, crafted using ultra-soft EnduraLuxe fabric to make it both incredibly light and incredibly durable, for added warmth.
