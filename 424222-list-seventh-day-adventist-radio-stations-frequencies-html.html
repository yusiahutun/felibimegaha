List of Seventh-Day Adventist Radio Stations and Their Frequencies

 Posted on August 14, 2024  |  9 minutes  |  1713 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Since the 1840s, the Seventh-Day Adventist Church has used many media ministries to spread the gospel. The media ministries have come in the form of print, radio, and television. Also, since the advancement of technology, the church has digital platforms, including the internet, to engage in its worldwide outreach. But of all forms of media used, the radio has played a significant role in reaching the masses. Today, there are literary thousands of Seventh-Day Adventist radio stations in all continents of the world.

Seventh-Day Adventist radio stations broadcast a Christian radio format of music and talk from studios around the globe to a network of AM and FM radio frequencies. Furthermore, some of these radio stations provide news programming covering economic, political, and religious topics.

What are the best Seventh-Day Adventist radio stations and their frequencies?

Having an array of subjects ensures all radio stations within the SDA Church cater to different people. But what are some of these SDA radio stations, and what are their frequencies? Here are some of the best global SDA radio stations to listen to today:

1. Adventist Radio Australia - 87.6 MHz

Adventist Radio Australia is a registered trademark of the Seventh-Day Adventist church. Its primary focus is to provide information on the location of its radio stations. In other words, Australians can search for a local ARA station by entering their postcode on the station’s website.

Generally, Adventist Radio Australia has many radio stations transmitting on different frequencies, depending on the location. In February 2008, Adventist Radio Australia started its first broadcast with Woodford 87.6 FM. Other stations are available in Lightning Ridge and Walgett.

2. Adventist Radio London

The Adventist Radio London first went live in 2018 in partnership with Adventist World Radio. Unlike other traditional radio stations, Adventist Radio London frequency is through Digital Audio Broadcasting. DAB is more efficient in its spectrum use than analogue FM radio and can offer more radio services for the given bandwidth.

The vision of Adventist Radio London is to empower its listeners to live in community, support them in their journey to holistic living and well-being, and encourage them to discover Jesus Christ as the only hope for humanity.

3. Baraton FM - 103.9 MHz

Baraton FM is one of the best SDA radio stations in Kenya. It is a Seventh-Day Adventist radio station owned by the University of Eastern Africa, Baraton and part of the UEAB media department.

The station broadcasts its programmes on 103.9 FM, which is only available in Nairobi, Kenya. Apart from radio, Baraton FM also broadcasts live videos on YouTube, Facebook, and other social media platforms. Visit the station’s website for more updates.

4. Ebenezer FM - 98.0 MHz

Ebenezer FM is a radio station in Uganda owned by the Uganda Union Mission, a church administrative unit of the Seventh-Day Adventist Church. The station broadcasts its programmes on 98.0 MHz in Mbale, Uganda. As an SDA radio station, Ebenezer dedicates all its topics to the mission of the church. Furthermore, the broadcaster promotes evangelism and ensures that its listeners grow spiritually.

5. Faith FM - 88.8 MHz

Faith FM is a radio station in Australia that features lifestyle and spirituality content related to the SDA Church. Some of the programmes it covers include religious music, family matters, and health issues. Biblical principles inspire all the topics that the broadcaster offers to its listeners. Faith FM broadcasts on 88.8 MHz Narrowcast.

It has many other frequencies, depending on a person’s location. For example, Armidale, Tennant Creek, Bowen, Barmera, Huonville, Lakes Entrance, and Esperance can listen to Faith FM on 87.6 FM. Visit the Faith FM website to find the frequencies for more areas in Australia.

6. Hope Channel Philippines

Hope Channel Philippines is a religious network of the Seventh-Day Adventist Church in the Philippines. The network runs many radio stations across the country on both AM and FM frequencies.

Its programmes reflect God’s love and promise to everyone, as demonstrated and manifested in people's lives. Some of the many SDA radio stations affiliated to Hope Channel Philippines and their frequencies include:

7. KSDA-FM - 91.9 MHz

KSDA-FM is a Seventh-Day Adventist Church radio station that transmits its programmes on 91.9 MHz and is the call sign for the radio station JOY FM located in Agana Heights, in the United States territory of Guam.

The station offers Christian stories and topics, as well as joyful music and Bible studies. Furthermore, it carries an excellent selection of audio dramas, preaching, and teaching programmes. The station only covers the island of Guam.

8. Kisima FM - 89.7 MHz

Kisima FM is a Kenyan Adventist radio station owned by the SDA Church in Nyamira Conference. The station broadcasts its services on 89.7 MHz, mostly reaching local people in the Greater Nyanza region.

Kisima FM covers many topics for its listeners. These include Bible verses and studies, religion, and theology. It also has an excellent music collection produced by some of the best SDA choirs in Kenya and abroad.

9. Radio Maranatha - 103.3 MHz

Radio Maranatha is a Seventh-Day Adventist radio and part of the Adventist World Radio. The radio broadcasts its services on 103.3 MHz. Its mission is to proclaim the everlasting gospel to every listener within its coverage.

As an SDA radio, it is owned by the Northern Zambia Union Conference located in Kabwe, Zambia. Some of the topics covered by the radio include Bible study, education, family life, spirituality, and stewardship.

10. Radio Salvation - 94.0 MHz

Radio Salvation is the official Seventh-Day Adventist radio station in Juba, South Sudan, that transmits its signals on 94.0 MHz, covering most areas of Juba. The South Sudan Attached Territory owns the station in the Greater Equatoria Field conference. Some of its topics range from education, family, and health to evangelism and stewardship. Furthermore, it offers an excellent mix of Christian music from across the globe.

11. Radio Tarumbeta - 90.4 MHz

Radio Tarumbeta is an SDA radio station in Kenya owned by the Ranen Conference, a Seventh-Day Adventist Church organisation. It uses the 90.4 MHz frequency that covers the localities of Awendo in Kenya. The radio presents itself as a medium where people can tune in to listen to the world of God and fulfil the commission of Jesus Christ.

12. Radio WZOL - 98.3 MHz

Radio WZOL is an SDA radio station located in San Juan, Porto Rico, owned by the Seventh-Day Adventists of Eastern Puerto Rico Association. The radio began broadcasting in 1978 on 98.9 FM. However, in 2013, it switched to 98.3 FM. WZOL also has broadcast translators with the call sign of W227CV on 93.3 FM. It covers the localities of San Lorenzo, Puerto Rico.

13. Sheki FM - 106.6 MHz

Sheki FM is a Christian radio located in Mombasa, Kenya, affiliated with the Seventh-Day Adventist Church's Kenya Coast Field organisational unit. It broadcasts its services on 106.6 FM, covering most areas of Mombasa. The mission of Sheki FM is to spread the gospel of Jesus in Kenya and help listeners understand the challenges faced in society today.

14. VOAR-FM - 96.7 MHz

VOAR-FM is one of the best Adventist radio stations in Canada that first went on air in 1929. It is a Christian radio that serves the St. John's metropolitan area on 96.7 FM. The radio airs a blend of Christian talk and teaching programmes along with Contemporary Christian music. Furthermore, it hosts many religious leaders to help in Bible study, preaching, and teachings.

15. Wikwatyo FM - 105.3 MHz

Wikwatyo FM is a radio station in Kenya that falls under the East Kenya Union Conference. It transmits its signals on 105.3 FM and covers the entire area of Kitui, Kenya. Its mission is to spread the gospel in Kenya and educate listeners on the values taught by Jesus. The radio also has a mix of the best Christian songs sourced globally.

16. WGTS - 91.9 MHz

AdveWGTS is a non-commercial radio station owned by Atlantic Gateway Communications Inc and affiliated with Washington Adventist University. The station broadcasts on 91.9 FM and operates a repeater service called WGBZ on 88.3 FM.
