  • His daughter Rita Field Marsham declined the one-fourteenth share given to her and instead donated it to other beneficiaries

The late Nicholas Biwott was a politician whose life was shrouded in mystery and secrecy.

Before he died in 2017, he was ranked among the richest Kenyans with investments across diverse sectors.

As reported by Business Daily, Ventures Financial magazine ranked him among Africa's top 50 billionaires.

During the KANU era under the late president Danie Arap Moi's leadership, he was nicknamed Total Man and wielded immense power.

Among his businesses and properties were Kenol-Kobil shares (now Rubis Energy), Premier Group of Companies, Ziba Management & Services., HZ Group of Companies in Israel and Lima Group.

Business Today reported that he also had shares in Safaricom, S.R Telecommunications Co. Ltd, which bagged Telkom Kenya contracts, National Milling Corporation and Trinity Enterprises.

Why did Rita Marsham refuse Biwott's wealth?

The daughter of the former powerful Cabinet minister made headlines for declining a share of the wealth allocated to her.

Marsham declined the one-fourteenth share given to her and instead donated it to other beneficiaries.

“I am named as a beneficiary of a share of the deceased’s estate. I wish to disclaim all my right, title, and interest to the share in the deceased’s estate," she said, as reported by Pulselive Kenya.

Marsham is the CEO of Knowledge Empowering Youth (KEY) and Charles Field-Marsham Foundation.

When did Nicholas Biwott die?

Biwott died on Tuesday, July 11, 2017.

On the morning that he died, the veteran politician had complained that he was feeling a little under the weather.

After taking his breakfast, Biwott told his family that he was going to the hospital but slumped to the floor before leaving the house.

He was pronounced dead upon arrival at the Nairobi Hospital. He was 77 years old at the time and had worked in different capacities for the first three Kenyan presidents.
