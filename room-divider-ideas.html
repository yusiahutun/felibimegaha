Slat Wall Room Divider

Liz Morrow Studios

Interior designer Liz Morrow built this slat divider to define separate areas without making her living space feel closed off. "I love how it allows the light to come through from the large windows in the dining room, but it still gives the living room a cozy feeling," she says.

Freestanding Shelves

Open shelving, such as an etagere, will preserve a spacious feel between "rooms," while a unit with a closed back will create privacy. Just make sure your shelves are stable enough to serve as a freestanding divider.

Salvaged Window Room Divider

Snazzy Home

Bring charm and history to your space with a salvaged window room divider. This one is super simple to DIY: Install two eye bolts on the window as attachment points, screw corresponding eye bolts into a ceiling beam, and hang with wire or chain. The window will delineate separate spaces while still letting light flow through.

See more from this room at Snazzy Home.
