From Edday to Karen Nyamu: Kenyan Celebs Celebrate Mother's Day with Heartwarming Messages

 Posted on July 13, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1268 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon
  • Kenyan celebrities took to social media to pen heartwarming messages to their mums and partners in their lives on Mother's Day
  • Some, like actor Baha and journalist Irene Muchuma, celebrated their late mums with tearjerking messages, reminiscing about the good old days
  • Singer Kambua, who lost her secondborn kid a few days after birth, penned a touching message to mums who've gone through the same

On May 14, Kenyan celebrities joined their counterparts across the world in celebrating Mother's Day.

Celebs share lovely Mother's Day messages

Many celebrities, including Grace Ekirapa, nominated senator Karen Nyamu, and Citizen TV presenter Willis Raburu celebrated Mother's Day with sweet messages.

From spouses, to mothers to mother-inlaws, before are messages from celebrities.

2. Willis Raburu

The Citizen TV presenter posted photos of his partner Ivy Namu and their kids thanking her for being a great mother to their children.

I love you we are all the better because you are in our lives. Happy Mother's Day namu tamu."

Samidoh's wife shared a photo with her three children at the New York City Times Square and wished mothers a happy Mother's Day.

7. Kambua

"Today is Mother’s Day. It’s a beautiful day for most, and a deeply painful one for others. To every woman who has desired to have a child, and still has an empty womb and expectant hands; to every woman who gave birth to a sleeping baby; to every woman who’s had to say goodbye to their baby; to every woman who has experienced the pain of a miscarr*age, Happy Mother’s Day. Happy Mother’s Day to all of you amazing women who have carried a child; to all who’ve become mothers through the miracle of adoption; to all Aunties who take care of nieces and nephews; to the Church moms, and every woman who plays the role of mom in one way or another."
