Robert Patrick's biography

The actor was born on November 5, 1958, in Marietta, Georgia, in the United States. He is 63 years old as of 2022, and his zodiac is Scorpio. He was raised in Marietta, Boston, Mass, Dayton, Ohio, Detroit, Michigan, and Cleveland.

His parents are Robert M. Patrick and Nadine, and he has Scots-Irish and English ancestry. Robert grew up in a family of six children whereby he was the eldest. His other siblings include Richard, Lewis, Karen, and Cheri Patrick.

His brother, Richard Patrick, is a well-known musician and is the lead singer of the rock band Filter, for which Robert has made appearances on special occasions. In his childhood, Robert hated acting and refused to wear a pair of green tights required for a Peter Pan character when he was in third grade.

He attended the Farmington High School in Farmington, Michigan, where he graduated in 1977. After that, he joined the Bowling Green State University and became a track and field and football athlete, but he dropped out before graduating.

Career

Before graduating from college, Robert decided to drop out to pursue drama and acting. He started by working as a house painter to support himself. He did this work until 1984 when he quit after a boating accident in Lake Erie. He had to swim for three hours to save those stranded on the accident site and almost drowned in doing so.

After the accident, Robert moved to Los Angeles at 26. He became a bartender and lived in his car as he searched for acting roles. He began his journey as an actor in low-budget science-fiction and action films made in the Philippines. He landed leading roles in pictures like Equalizer 2000 and Eye of the Eagle.

The B movies he had been a part of in the Philippines propelled him to get a SAG card and get noticed in Hollywood. His first major Hollywood film was Die Hard 2, but he landed a small part as a henchman for Colonel Stuart. He was brought into the limelight when he landed the role of the T-1000, which was the villain of Terminator 2: Judgment Day (1991).
