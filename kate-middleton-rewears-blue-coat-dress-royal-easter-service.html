Kate Middleton Rewears Blue Coat Dress for Royal Easter Service

 Posted on March 21, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  365 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Catherine, Princess of Wales, wore her signature style in a striking shade of blue for this year's royal Easter tradition.

She joined the royal family at St. George's Chapel for Easter Mattins Service this morning, arriving at the church with Prince William and all three of their children: Prince George, Princess Charlotte, and Prince Louis. For the occasion, the Wales family coordinated in shades of blue, with Kate bringing back a royal-blue Catherine Walker coat that she previously wore for Commonwealth Service in March 2022.

The royal paired the piece with a new matching hat by Lock and Co., which featured a thin, oversized bow. She completed the look with a pair of nude pumps, a royal-blue clutch, and gold-and-sapphire drop earrings.

Prince William and Prince George wore matching navy suits for this morning's service, with young Prince Louis making his royal Easter debut in a navy suit jacket with light blue shorts, knee-high socks, and black dress shoes. Meanwhile, Princess Charlotte layered a navy coat over a blue-and-white floral dress, adding a pair of bright blue stockings and black Mary Jane flats.

WPA Pool//Getty Images

WPA Pool//Getty Images

This year, the royal family celebrated their first Easter since Queen Elizabeth II's death, with the holiday also falling on the two-year anniversary of Prince Philip's death. King Charles III and Queen Camilla led the procession of senior royals arriving to today's service, ahead of next month's coronation ceremony.

Other senior royals who attended this year's service include Princess Anne and Sir Timothy Lawrence; Prince Andrew; Prince Edward and Sophie, Countess of Wessex, with their son James, Viscount Severn; Princess Beatrice and her husband Edoardo Mapelli Mozzi; Princess Eugenie and her husband Jack Brooksbank; Peter Philips; and Zara and Mike Tindell and their daughters, Mia and Lena.
