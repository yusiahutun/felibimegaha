Racy streamer blackmailed with AI fakes hit with seventh Twitch ban after 'camel toe'

 Posted on May 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  455 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

A racy streamer who was previously blackmailed by fans using artificial intelligence has been hit with her seventh Twitch ban.

X account StreamerBans confirmed the suspension of JenFoxxx, real name Jenelle Dagres, earlier today, with the adult entertainer temporarily barred from using the streaming service. No official comment has been made by the streamer.

JenFoxx, previously registered on the platform as IndieFoxx, is no stranger to bans, with a previous controversial removal from the platform coming after a "camel toe" fiasco. A reason for the current ban has not been released.

A ban record showed JenFoxxx was banned on March 21, 2024, with a previous ban received on June 29, 2021, lasting two years. But the star returned to the platform after her exile from the platform was lifted on April 5, 2023.

Twitch has strict policies on sexual or suggestive content outlined in its community guidelines, which read: "We don’t permit streamers to be fully or partially nude, including exposing genitals or buttocks. We do not permit the visible outline of genitals, even when covered.

"For all streamers, you must cover the area extending from your hips to the bottom of your pelvis and buttocks."

From then, the star promised racy content and a return which saw fans celebrating the platform allowing JenFoxxx to create content once more. The content creator posed in a black and neon pink two-piece, accented with silver chains and a gold Cartier love bracelet.

But her streams were not without controversy, with Twitch's slackening policy on hot tub streams landing the star in hot water. Jenelle previously claimed she was booted off the platform for an accidental "camel toe".

Speaking on a podcast about the incident, Jenelle said the website penalised her for "intentionally showing [her] genitals". Jenelle later found herself blackmailed by fraudsters who used artificial intelligence to deepfake nude photos of the star.

She took to X and wrote: "So today, someone tried to blackmail me for money, threatening to leak AI-generated nudes of me, seriously."

Fans were quick to reply to Jenelle, who told her to report the incident to authorities and that there were "lots of scammers out on the internet nowadays."
