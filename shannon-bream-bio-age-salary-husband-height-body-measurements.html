Even as a child Shannon was interested in journalism and news. Her inspiration for a career in this field came from visiting beautiful journalists and newsreaders and although her parents didn’t approve of her ambition because they thought she would never make ends meet with a career in journalism she managed to prove them wrong.

The successful journalist began her academic journey at a primary school in her hometown of Tallahassee, Florida, and then moved to North Florida Christian High School in Tallahassee where she graduated in 1988 at the age of 17.

Moving on to college Shannon graduated in business administration from Liberty University in Lynchburg Virginia in 1992.

During her studies in 1990, she participated in the Miss Virginia Beauty Contest and was named a winner. She went one step further by participating in the Miss America contest in 1991 although she did not win Shannon was one of the top 10 finalists this year. Her scholarship took care of much of her education.

After graduating from Liberty University in 1993 Shannon returned to Tallahassee and attended Florida State University Law School. During her studies at Florida State Law School, she won the Miss Florida USA Competition (1995) and participated in the Miss USA Competition (1995) which took fourth place.

Shannon graduated from Florida State University College of Law in 1996 and moved to Tampa Florida where she began her legal career. But somehow her childish ambition to become a journalist never died so she eventually made a career change from a lawyer to a career in the television news.

Shannon moved to Charlotte North Carolina in 2001 and became an evening and late news reporter for CBS subsidiary WBTV. After three years with WBTV, she joined NBC’s WRC-TV subsidiary in Washington D.C… There she was a weekend thanksgiver and assumed general responsibilities.

While still at WRC-TV she met Brit Hume editor-in-chief of the Fox News Channel – Washington Bureau. He encouraged her to submit audio samples to Fox News and she joined the network in November 2007. Shannon is based at the Fox News Channel office in Washington DC.
