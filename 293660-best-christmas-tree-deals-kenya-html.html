Christmas is here with us, again, and you know what that means – the carols, the jingles and the Christmas tree! Talking about that, I remember when I was still kid – many, many years ago, my brothers and I would spend the entire day in some nearby bush looking for a nice cypress to chop down for our Christmas tree. We’d then drag it all the way, and place it in the furthest corner, leaving needles all over the place. Yes, it was quite a hassle, but trust me, the joy of erecting and decorating it with some colored papers and balloons was well worth it.

I know not so many of us are keen on the reality of dragging a 6ft cypress through their home – not when there’s an easy, and equally beautiful option in the market. If you count yourself among this group, then it may be time to hop online and find the best tree for the holidays. So, without further ado, here is our tree-mendous guide to finding the best Christmas tree deals in Kenya.

Why buy an artificial Christmas tree?

The biggest advantage is cost: these trees can serve you for decades, so they are considerably cheaper than buying a real tree each year. Top retailers in Kenya like OLX, Jumia and Masoko have incredible deals at the moment. Masoko has 13.33% off. OLX, has also listed a range of low-priced trees from different retailers around the country.

Beauty-wise, for those of you who are obsessed with symmetry, the artificial trees are a perfect choice because they look tremendous from all angles – no worries about trimming, or positioning. But most importantly, you won’t have to hoover up pesky needles, as we did.

  • Size: Typically, Christmas trees range between 2ft and 15 ft. A majority of people prefer the 7ft tree, which is perfect for an average sized living space. But you can go for a bigger or smaller one for that matter, depending on your preference.
  • Price: At Masoko, a 2ft Christmas tree goes for a special price of KES. 1,400 while a 15ft Christmas tree costs KES 45,027. OLX also offers incredible deals on the same. You can get a fully decorated 7ft Christmas tree at KES. 6,500!
  • PE or PVC: Christmas trees aren’t created equal, some have better quality than others. Those that are designed from PE look more realistic, compared to the PVC ones. However, the PE Christmas trees cost more.
