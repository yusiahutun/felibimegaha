Biography

Ferruccio Lamborghini was born in Italy on April 28, 1916. Entrepreneur and businessman who started in oil heaters and air conditioning then went on to become the creator of the luxurious Lamborghini sports cars. He was fascinated with bullfighting and actually named several models after bulls. One of these was the Islero, which was the name of the bull who killed Manolete. He was captured by the British during WW II and put to work in their motor department. He used his newly-gained knowledge to make tractors for local farmers in Italy after the war.
