Marty Smith is a well-known name in the world of sports. He stands out as one of ESPN’s most celebrated and respected hosts and reporters. Among other things, he reports for ESPN on several sporting events such as car racing, basketball, and soccer.

Marty joined ESPN in 2006. Since then he has taken on several mature roles in sports television and has reported on platforms such as ESPN.com and SportsCenter. Marty also has his own show called Marty Smith’s America, which was launched in 2017. Learn more about the journalist in this article.

Marty Smith – Bio

The ESPN reporter is from Pearisburg, Virginia, in the United States. He was born in 1976, exactly on April 15th. Not much is known about his childhood, but Smith is reported to have developed a fondness for sporting events, especially football, at a young age.

The American journalist received his high school education at the prestigious Giles High School in Virginia, after which he was accepted at Radford University to continue his studies. During his time in high school, Marty Smith was a member of the basketball, baseball, and soccer teams at Giles High School. He was a member of the Giles soccer team that won the 1993 State Championship.

Before his fame, the sports reporter worked as a sports analyst for daily newspapers in Lynchburg and Roanoke. He was then hired as a senior writer for NASCAR.com, a NASCAR analyst at Fox Sports Net.

Smith’s journalistic career moved to a higher level after joining ESPN as a NASCAR reporter in 2006. From 2007 to 2014, the journalist led ESPN’s NASCAR reporting program for NASCAR Now, NASCAR Countdown, and SportsCenter.

Throughout his flourishing career, Marty’s program included one-on-one interviews with sporting elites such as Holywood-born pro golfer Rory McIlroy, Portuguese soccer player Christiano Ronaldo, Palmdale basketball pro-Paul George, and other stars outside the sports community, including American singer-songwriter Sam Hunt.

In addition to his day job, the journalist has also toured the world at various events. Some time ago, Marty spent a week in Rome alongside the University of Michigan soccer team and popular soccer coach James Joseph Harbaugh.

2017 was a great year for the sports reporter, as it was the year he launched his own show called Marty Smith’s America. In addition to the show running on ESPN2, Marty is also the host of Marty Smith’s America podcast.

Parents and Brother 

The ESPN reporter does not normally talk about himself in front of the camera. On one occasion, however, he got personal with some fans and told how his father took him to games at Lane Stadium when he was a boy.

Smith’s father, Leo Smith, died in 2008, and before his death he was a big fan of Virginia Tech soccer, having graduated from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University. The journalist’s mother’s name is Joy Massey Smith. She died of cancer in 1998.

The ESPN presenter has always called car racer Davey Allison his brother and childhood hero. Davey, a popular driving legend, died in a plane crash on July 13, 1993, at the age of 32. He was recently inducted into the NASCAR Hall of Fame.
