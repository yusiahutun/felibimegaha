American Psycho on Broadway - American Psycho on Broadway Review

 Posted on May 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  324 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Did you guys know Lady Gaga premiered her latest music video last night? It's a live-action feature, approximately two and a half hours long, showing eight times a week at the Schoenfeld Theatre and is titled, akin to her other pursuit American Horror Story, American Psycho.

Emphasis on the "psych," though. We're just kidding! Sadly, the high priestess of spectacle is not on view for a multiple-month stint in our backyard. Rather, it's the cast of the musical American Pyscho, a high-powered, designer-brand footnote to the 2000 movie version starring Christian Bale and the novel, published by Bret Easton Ellis in 1991. Gaga has no affiliation, but all jokes aside, the entire production reads like one long MTV video inspired by her. With music by Duncan Sheik (Spring Awakening) and set design by Es Devlin (Remember that super slick Adele concert set for her big welcome back concert? That was hers.), this is a performance that's equal parts weird and wonderful.

Jeremy Daniel

We all know the infamous premise: a serial killer (Patrick Bateman, played half cheekily/half chillingly by Benjamin Walker), who, driven by the monotony yet maniacal pace and big bucks bromances in late-1980s Manhattan, begins to unravel at the thought of failure and fatigue and begins a spree of drawing blood from beautiful ladies (clad in vintage Chanel, Valentino and Jean Paul Gaultier, to-boot). Such a formula is not abnormal to Broadway (Sweeney Todd anyone?), but the songs are more lyrically ironic and nostalgic than melodic, and they take a stab at the big themes of a decade defined by capitalism and greed--even name-dropping Donald Trump. We did mention this is American Psycho, right?

Patrick keeps those around him—and the audience—guessing what monstrosities lurk in his mind till the very last note. But in the lineup of musicals this season, of one conviction you can be certain: American Psycho slays.

Jeremy Daniel

American Psycho is now open at the Schoenfeld Theatre.
