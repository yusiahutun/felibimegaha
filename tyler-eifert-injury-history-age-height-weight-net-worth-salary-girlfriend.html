Tyler Eifert Bio, Age

Tyler Gregory Eifert was born on September 8, 1990, in Fort Wayne, Indiana. He attended Saint Vincent De Paul Catholic School and Bishop Dwenger High School – all in Fort Wayne, Indiana. Most of the athletes are known for being active in high school sports. At Bishop Dwenger High School, Eifert played football and had 9 touchdowns on offense, 97 tackles, and 5 interceptions on defense. After graduating he was enrolled at the University of Notre Dame and played for the school’s team known as the Fighting Irish Football Team.

Eifert played in the 2010 season with 27 receptions for 352 yards and 2 touchdowns in 11 games. He was named a finalist for the 2011 Mackey Award for breaking his team’s record, which was previously held by Ken MacAfee as the record for the most individual receptions and reception places. After winning the Mackey Award in 2012, he decided to play in the professional league and gave up his senior year. During the 2013 NFL Draft, Eifert was selected 21st in the first round by the Cincinnati Bengals and signed a four-year contract worth $8.25 million.

Career and Injury History

Tyler Eifert has a long history of injuries that began for him as a younger athlete. He suffered a back injury in 2009 when he was a freshman in college, which meant that he only played one game all season. After joining the Cincinnati Bengals, he recorded five receptions for 47 yards in his very first game against the Chicago Bears. In his next game against the Pittsburg Steelers, he caught three passes for 66 yards and made a very long catch, considered his longest of the season. In December of the same year, he suffered a stinger injury and was unable to participate in the last game of the season.

In the 2014 season, Eifert again suffered a dislocated elbow caused by a tackle and stayed out of the game throughout the season to recover. In the 2015 season, he came back stronger and led his team to victory over the Oakland Raiders with nine 104-yard receptions and two touchdowns. After defeating the Baltimore Ravens and winning the 2016 Pro Bowl, a game presented and sponsored by the United Services Automobile Association, he played for Team Irvin in 2016 but injured his ankle.

In the same year, he again suffered from back pain and underwent three surgeries, two for his persistent back pain, and the other for the removal of a cyst from his knee. The injuries he suffered severely affected his career. Instead of signing a long-term contract, he signed a one-year contract to stay with his team on March 15, 2018.
