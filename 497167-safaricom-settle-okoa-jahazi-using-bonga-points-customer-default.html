Safaricom to Settle Okoa Jahazi Using Bonga Points if Customer Defaults

 Posted on April 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  393 words  |  Martina Birk
  • The Okoa Jahazi service enables eligible prepay subscribers to opt to receive an advance credit facility every time their airtime is below KSh 5
  • The new move by the giant telco is meant to reduce the value of Bonga points yet to be redeemed by customers worth billions of shillings
  • Defaulters have already received messages notifying them of deductions of their Bonga points with each point valued at 30 cents

Safaricom customers who default on their Okoa Jahazi will have their Bonga points deducted automatically to repay the debt.

The new move by the giant telco is meant to reduce the value of Bonga points, yet to be redeemed by customers worth billions of shillings.

Safaricom's unredeemed Bonga Points revenue hit KSh 4.5 billion as of March 2022, up from KSh 3 billion recorded in 2015.

How Okoa Jahazi works

According to Safaricom, the Auto Okoa Service enables eligible prepay subscribers to opt to receive an Okoa Jahazi credit facility every time their airtime is below KSh 5.

The company charges an advance fee of 10% for each request. The service is accessed by dialling *131#.

"The balance of the Auto Okoa Jahazi debt must be fully repaid within five days from the date of issue failing which you will be blacklisted for 24hrs," the telco stated.

Defaulters deducted Bonga points

Business Daily reported that defaulters had already received messages notifying them of deductions of their Bonga points.

"Dear customer, we have deducted 2,500 Bonga points for KSh 750 to repay your Okoa loan,” one message to a customer read.

Safaricom places the value of each point at 30 cents.

Safaricom rules outs imposing expiry date on Bonga points

In January, Safaricom PLC confirmed that there was no announcement detailing the expiry period for its loyalty programme Bonga.

“At no point did Safaricom announce that Bonga (Points) were expiring,” read the firm's response on the matter.

Subscribers continued to access their Bonga points accrued before 2019, despite reports that the firm could scrap them.

There were reports of the changes in the programme's terms of service published by the firm in November 2022.

This saw a Nakuru-based customer move to court seeking to block the firm from implementing the changes.
