10 best guitarists in Kenya that you should know about today

Beats combine various musical instruments, which, if done just right, produce the masterpiece you jam to in your favourite song. The guitar is one musical instrument that is pivotal in music. As much as the instrument is integral, it would sound nothing out of the ordinary without a talented player. Therefore, it takes the hands of a skilled guitarist to bring out the best in the guitar. And the best guitarists in Kenya have shown the country how it is done. So, who is the best?

Numerous musicians have revolutionised music and defined their respective genres. Conversely, guitarists have also harnessed the skill and entertained music listeners in different capacities.

Often, they are in the background, hardly in the limelight, but some are so good that they have broken the barrier to become fan favourites. How many guitarists have achieved centre stage in Kenya?

Would you attend a guitarist’s concert? It hardly happens in Kenya, but things change. The most iconic guitarists give fans life-changing experiences as they satiate their appetite for good tunes from the old-school instrument that has existed for thousands of years. But who counts as the best guitarist in Kenya?

Considering the country's diverse music genres, there have been mixed reactions towards guitarists; some are considered the best in their respective fronts and wouldn’t even be considered in others. However, according to People Daily, these are the best guitarists in Kenya.

1. Polycarp Otieno

It is only recently that fans have heard Polycarp Otieno sing. He has been part of Sauti Sol since the band's formation, but fans hardly listened to his voice except for interviews. Like many people, you have probably wondered what kept him in the award-winning group.

He is an immensely talented guitarist. Polycarp’s exceptional guitar skills have earned him the name Fancy Fingers, a moniker he uses often. Over the years, the guitarist has grown from being Sauti Sol’s exclusive guitarist to playing other music blends, including rhumba, blues-rock, and much more.

2. Ivy Alexander

Is Ivy Alexander the best guitarist in Africa? He could be, as her skills are unmatched in Kenya. The Nairobi-based guitarist gained fame from the Flower Project, an all-female contemporary band. It was founded in 2013 by OneBeat Fellow Kasiva Mutua and served as the house band for Coke Studio Africa.

Ivy Alexander won the Guitar Guru Award at the Nairobi Guitar Festival. During her early days in her career, Ivy often played the guitar for gospel and neo-soul music, which was as impressive as how she plays today.

3. Kato Change

Kato Change is one of the few top guitarists in Kenya to have toured different destinations worldwide. He has toured Brazil on other occasions and even played in numerous non-continental music shows like Coke Studio. Kato has also performed and produced music with great names like Salif Keita, Aloe Blac, and Luedji Luna.

Something else about Kato Change is that he has paved the way for other guitarists in the music industry via his projects. One of his most phenomenal projects is The Change Experience, which has significantly impacted his peers in the music industry.

4. Jack Muguna

Jack Muguna is a jazz guitarist who has played for the Nairobi Horns Project, among others. He is the co-founder of the Sarabi and Muguna Music Group. The guitarist's debut album, Journey of the Soul, was released in March 2021.

Jack is underrated but well-respected among musicians and jazz connoisseurs. He quietly built his impressive reputation over the years, and his music is rooted in offering a personality.

5. Kevin Munyi

Kevin Munyi started his musical journey at only seven when he undertook the cello. And the most exciting thing is that he had already taught himself how to play the keyboard by the time he was eleven. He took the guitar when he was twelve and fell in love with it. Today, Kevin is one of the best guitarists in Kenya.

6. AcouSlyk

AcouSlyk is a gifted Kenyan guitarist that has made a name for himself in the music industry. He is purely self-taught and has been playing for quite some time. AcouSlyk started playing the guitar after high school and fumbled through it without a tutor or music theory.

The guitarist's style of playing is referred to as fingerstyle, which is one guitarist producing the sound of the entire band.

7. Robin Marcel Moustache

Robin’s excellent skills have seen him get respected in the music industry, where he is also a pioneer. The skilled guitarist is the co-founder of the Nairobi Guitar Festival, which has aided in creating a platform where other upcoming instrumentalists to interact and grow together.

Marcel is the lead guitarist of the Tha Movement Band and is also one of East Africa’s best reggae groups, The Roots Connection.

8. Tugi Mlamba

Tuigi is a multitalented guitarist from Nairobi, Kenya. He started playing musical instruments when he was young and has worked with bands like Afrology, Ricky na Marafiki, Mwai the Truth, and Joseph Helon.

Tugi always has an energetic stage presence, which excites fans who grace his shows. He is also a rising songwriter as he tries to venture into other genres of the music industry. Tugi is a studio guitarist for famous personalities like Nviiri The Storyteller.

9. Shyam Shama

Shyma Shama represents what persistence and dedication yield. The guitarist has been successful in areas where not many would expect a Kenyan-born Indian. He founded Orchestra Masika Band and has an impressive profile in the music industry, especially among guitarists. Shyma’s now based in the United Kingdom, still pursuing his love for rhumba.

10. Evans Mutanduki

Evans didn’t become one of the top Kenyan guitarists from sheer luck but through hard work and dedication. The guitarist is passionate about his trade and expresses his craft with massive allure.
