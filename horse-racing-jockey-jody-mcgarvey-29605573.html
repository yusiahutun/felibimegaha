Jockey screams in agony as horse kicks out at him before race in the parade ring

 Posted on June 18, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  338 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Jockey Jody McGarvey screamed in agony as he was kicked by his horse in a horrifying moment just before a race.

The Irishman was just about to ride Emotional Damage - but the five-year old mare inflicted serious physical damage before her run at Naas on Thursday.

Footage shows the Gordon Elliott-trained horse rear up and lash out at McGarvey, appearing to strike the unfortunate jockey in the calf as he looked to mount her on the parade ring - with the horse seemingly spooked.

And McGarvey's participation in the Irish Stallion Farms EBF Mares Maiden Hurdle looked in serious doubt when the Derry native hobbled off clutching his leg in serious pain.

But the brave rider only suffered minor damage and recovered in time to take to the racecourse in County Kildare.

Unfortunately, McGarvey could not ride the mare to a dream victory, coming home in ninth place in a race won by favourite Troubled Times.

But the jockey was able to see the funny side of a dangerous moment after a clip of the incident was shared on Twitter by horse racing pundit Kevin Blake.

Responding to the footage, McGarvey replied: "Got a little kick in the a**e today!" And fans were quick to praise McGarvey for his courage as he continued despite being caught by a swinging hoof.

"Looked pretty painful, well done for carrying on," one said, while another agreed: "Fair play to you for carrying on." Others rather appropriately replied: "Ouch."

McGarvey had earlier been in action on board Milk Market, riding the Padraig Roche-trained horse to a 12th place finish at the The Naas Racecourse Business Club Maiden Hurdle.
