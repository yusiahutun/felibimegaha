Moment thief brazenly nicks rail passenger's 1,400 bicycle at train station - amid spate of muggin

 Posted on March 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  525 words  |  Martina Birk

This is the moment a bike thief was caught out by a train's CCTV cameras after he stole a commuter's £1,400 wheels from a train carriage. 

Benjamin Hendy, 41, nipped onto the train at Huntingdon station and grabbed the expensive fold-up bike from the area close to the doors, where it had been stored for the journey. 

Weeks earlier Hendy was also caught on CCTV cutting the lock to a £550 bike in a public street, it later emerged.

Hendy was captured on film snipping the lock in Princess Street, Huntingdon, on September 5.

Footage shows Benjamin Hendy, 41, stealing a commuter's £1,400 bike from a stopped train carriage

Hendy nipped onto the train and grabbed the expensive fold-up bike from the area close to the doors

Hendy was caught on CCTV stealing the bike while the train was stopped at Huntingdon station 

He stole the bike worth £1,400 on September 20 from the train which had stopped at Huntingdon train station.

Hendy was sentenced to 30 weeks in prison at Cambridge Magistrates' Court after pleading guilty to two counts of shop theft and two counts of bike theft.

The sentence also activated a previous suspended sentence.

PC Louise Plumridge, who investigated for Cambridgeshire Police, said: 'For many people a bike can be a lifeline as their only method of transport and/or their tool for exercise.

'We recognise the impact these thefts can have on people's lives, and we're committed to catching those responsible and putting them before the courts.'

It comes as terrified cyclists have been ditching high-end Brompton bikes amid a spate of violent muggings by gangs who hide in park bushes and stalk riders on mopeds.

Shocking video footage from recent months shows a series of brazen attacks in which thugs knock innocent cyclists off their bikes as well as pinching them from gardens and outside shops

Shocking video footage from recent months capture a series of brazen attacks in which thugs knock innocent cyclists off their bikes, and steal them from gardens and outside shops. 

It came amid a Met Police warning that Albanian gangs are stealing expensive bikes from the streets of the UK before sending them to Russia to be sold.

Cycling groups said the criminals have a 'list' of expensive makes to target, including those made by S-Works and Pinarello. 

Brompton bikes - which range in price from £899 to £4,180 - are also being targeted to such an extent that rentals have declined amid growing fears they make users a target, a cycling chief told MailOnline. 

Another group leader said cyclists are now covering up high-end brand logos to avoid being targeted, while enthusiasts on forums say they are disguising their bikes or switching to cheaper alternatives.

A spokesperson for Brompton told MailOnline the attacks were 'concerning' and called on the police to 'do more to keep Londoners safe on their bikes'.
