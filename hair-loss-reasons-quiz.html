    Excess hair loss affects 50% of women by the age of 50, meaning that 8 million women in the UK are currently experiencing the condition. Discovering hair loss, thinning or breakage can feel alarming, but many of the causes can be treated once correctly identified. 

    Thinning hair might be triggered by your diet, lifestyle, hormonal imbalances or milestones such as post-partum or the menopause. Stress can also play a key part, producing more cortisol which attacks the immune system and thus ‘switches off’ the hair follicle as your body goes into survival mode. Interrupted sleep will also impact hair shedding and loss.

    Oestrogen levels drop both after childbirth and during the menopause, with hair entering the ‘resting’ phase where it may fall out and feel thinner. In cases of post-partum, a woman can generally expect normal hair growth to return within nine months of giving birth, and for menopause-related cases, HRT could prove helpful to rebalance hormone levels.
