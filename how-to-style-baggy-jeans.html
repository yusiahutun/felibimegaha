9 Baggy Jeans Outfit Ideas That Are Totally Wearable

 Posted on February 6, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  639 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In the world of fashion, there's a small yet powerful group that considers being comfortable just as important as looking cute. They'll skip the heels in favor of sneakers, steer clear of shirts with too many straps and ties, and prefer their jeans to be loose-fitting rather than tight and restricting (ahem, skinny jeans).

Still, when it comes to figuring out how to wear baggy jeans, there is room for error. For instance, if you're hoping to avoid looking too casual, you might want to skip the oversized sweatshirt. At the same time, not everyone wants to wear a body-hugging crop top to balance things out. Fortunately, there's another way, a better way, in fact. Ahead, we've rounded up nine outfit ideas for how to style baggy jeans that will leave you inspired. With these quick solutions, you'll be ready for anything and won't even miss your sweats ... that much.

Keep scrolling for our favorite baggy jeans outfits you'll want to break out all year long.

With a Layered Turtleneck

Business casual just got a whole new makeover. A wrap top and layered turtleneck perfectly complement a pair of baggy jeans. Make sure to cuff your pant leg for a more polished finish.

With a Hoodie

It's the ultimate solution if your goal is casual-cool. Add a handful of bold accessories, such as a big-buckle belt or layered necklaces to give this classic style some personal flair.

With a Puffer Coat

It's essentially the opposite of the jellyfishing trend, but this oversized combo gives off a nostalgic '90s vibe that's perfect for today. Style your outdoor-friendly ensemble with a form-fitting shirt underneath so as not to get lost under all those layers.

With a Button-Down

Give baggy jeans a structured and somewhat preppy twist with the help of a collared shirt. While a crisp white something can do no wrong, a bright hue will help make your look pop. A pair of loafers or even babydoll shoes will work to pull it all together.

With Summer Staples

Baggy jeans are pretty airy. Translation? They're great to have in your wardrobe rotation year-round, including during the warmer months. For not-too-hot spring and summer days, reach for a cropped light-wash option and wear it with an eyelet top and chunky sandals for a sweet seasonal look.

With a Crop Top

OK, we know you saw this one coming, but it was just too good to pass up. A crop top, like this patterned number with puff sleeves, lends a fun and flirty feel to casual-leaning baggy pants. For an extra dose of nostalgia and color, don't overlook the scrunchie bracelet detail.

With a Blazer

A structured jacket has the ability to amp up the comfiest clothes and elevate any lazy day look — especially when combined with a bralette underneath. Celebs love this style, so we know you will, too.

With a Graphic Tee

Go for a laid-back grunge vibe with the help of a well-worn, vintage graphic tee. If the choice feels a little too casual or oversized, give your outfit a DIY touch — and show off more of your shape — by cropping or knotting your top to your liking. A statement jacket and heels will really set this look apart.

As an All-Black Outfit

Compared to slim-fitting denim, black baggy jeans will give this popular monochromatic combination a boost. The result is a unique, exciting ensemble that's deserving of a double take.

Tucked Into Boots

While you might be tempted to let your baggy jeans hang loose, tucking your pair into knee-high boots will create an eye-catching and playful shape — not to mention, show off your footwear.
