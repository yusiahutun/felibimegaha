Andrew Dice Clay isn't letting divorce get in the way love.

The '80s icon and his 31-year-old wife Valerie Vasquez have filed for divorce after four years of marriage, but are remaining in a romantic relationship.

The 56-year-old Blue Jasmine star and his makeup artist spouse swear that they are in fact 'more in love than ever,' according to People.

'We're more in love than ever before': Andrew Dice Clay and wife Valerie Vasquez - pictured here at the LA Blue Jasmine premiere in LA in 2013 - have filed for divorce but will remain in a romantic relationship together

Divorce papers filed in the Los Angeles Superior Court note that the couple separated on March 18.

'The word "marriage" was putting a pressure on our relationship,' Clay said in a statement to TMZ. 'And since we filed, we've been more in love and have had more respect for each other than ever before.'

The star - who portrayed Augie in Woody Allen's latest film - didn't let the filing pass them by without a party, either. 'We celebrated the divorce at Craig's in (West) Hollywood,' he declared.

Still kicking: Clay had a supporting role as Augie in Woody Allen's latest flick Blue Jasmine alongside Cate Blanchett, Alec Baldwin and Sally Hawkins

Clay has never been one to bow down to social conventions, littering his Twitter account with profanities and details about his sex life with Valerie.

A tweet on April 4 appeared to confirm that things were just peachy between he and Valerie - who changed her last name to Silverstein in honour of her husband's real last name.

'My Babe Valerie is not a Porn Star, Never was,' he wrote on the social networking site. 'Just acts like one. Just 4ME!!!'

'The word "marriage" was putting a pressure on our relationship': The 56-year-old actor said in a statement that the contractual nature of the relationship wasn't working out

Clay married Valerie in a Las Vegas wedding on Valentines day in 2010.

Back then, he gushed over their love to the Las Vegas Sun, saying: 'I've never felt this way before.'

'She is a gift from heaven. God knew we were meant for each other and planned exactly how and when we’d meet.

It was Clay's third time at the altar, as he was previously married to Kathy Swanson, and later Kathleen Monica, with whom he had two children.

Meanwhile, the actor and comedian has a memoir coming out later this year, entitled The Ugly Truth.
