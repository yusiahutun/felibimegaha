Country crooner Marcus King ties the knot with Briley Hussey in a lavish ceremony in Nashville

 Posted on January 28, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  858 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Marcus King is off the market, and he has a new queen. 

The country crooner, 26, married his fiancée Briley Hussey, 31, in a lavish ceremony Sunday at the Schermerhorn Symphony Center in Nashville. 

The Hard Working Man singer popped the question nearly a year ago, but said it was love at first sight as he described the bold way his future bride introduced herself in an interview with People.

'She waltzed up on my bus like she owned it, and I was taken with her sweet southern drawl. She asked to connect to the Bluetooth, blared Linda Ronstadt and Aretha Franklin and we sang and danced till it was time for the bus to leave. I told her the next morning to quit her job and marry me instead.'

The influencer did just that and the pair have been together ever since. 

I do: Country crooner Marcus King, 26, and Briley Hussey, 31, tied the knot Sunday in a lavish ceremony in Nashville officiated by Country Music Hall of Fame artist Jamey Johnson 

For the big day, Briley walked down the aisle is a regal looking long sleeve lace and taffeta gown designed by Rita Vinieris with a high waist and A-line skirt. 

Videos and photos shared in her Instagram Stories show the gorgeous bride's long brunette locks were styled in a low bun at the nape of the neck . She wore a long cathedral veil.

Because the dress boasted a high neck, Briley kept her jewelry simple, choosing large round diamond encrusted earrings from Kit Vintage.

The groom looked handsome in custom black tuxedo designed by Jukebox Mama, and white cowboy boots. 

The Grammy nominated artist kept true to his personal style wearing a new white cowboy over his long, curly hair. 

The music inspired ceremony included some personal touches. 

'Marcus has called me 'little bird' since the night we met,' Briley explained. The lyrics begin, 'We got married on a Sunday,' and the couple asked artist Leah Blevins to perform her song at the ceremony. 

The officiant was Country Music Hall of Fame artist Jamey Johnson who collaborated with Marcus and the Zac Brown Band on new rendition of Stubborn Pride last year.

After the ceremony, guests were invited to a sit down dinner that included, winter pear salad, Jack Daniel's barrel smoked short rib, sage crusted chicken, and pistachio and orange pan-roasted halibut. 

The newlyweds entered the room as a marching band played Hey Baby! Will You Be My Girl. 

The couple kept it old school when they shared their first dance, twirling around on a black and white checked floor to Mel Carter's 1965 hit Hold Me, Thrill Me, Kiss Me, as their guests looked on. 

Briley later let her hair down and changed into a white strapless reception dress with boa feather sleeves. Marcus switched from his black jacket to a white dinner jacket with black trim.
