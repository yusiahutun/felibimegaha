Spotlight on Ashley Longshore

 Posted on May 2, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  328 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

"As the creator of my artworks, I can explain and sell them better than anyone else," says the artist Ashley Longshore, who has used online and social-media platforms to introduce her work to a wider audience.

Longshore, who is known for her brightly coloured, often irreverent paintings inspired by pop culture, was an early adopter of Instagram as a way of making art more accessible. "I love the exploratory nature of social media; how it allows art collectors to make new discoveries," she says. "It brings my followers directly into my world, so I can show them the colour palettes I like and the things that inspire me." It's a valuable commercial tool, too; Longshore admits that she "can post a $30,000 [about £25,000] painting and sell it in minutes to a collector anywhere in the world".

Instagram is just one of the strands of Longshore's forward-thinking approach to selling art; she also runs her innovative Artgasm subscription service, whereby paid-up members receive four exclusive, limited-edition prints hand-signed by the artist each year. "It's a way for me to approach an audience of people who maybe couldn't afford to buy an original painting worth tens of thousands," explains Longshore.

Her partnerships with commercial brands, which have included the design of an extremely successful homeware range for Anthropologie, are a vital aspect of her broader identity as an artist-entrepreneur. "Artists aren't just an afterthought in these kinds of creative collaborations – we're businesspeople too," she points out.

Whether she is designing a piece of furniture or producing an artwork that explores important philosophical question (the unifying theme of her paintings is, she says, "the question of what it means to be a woman in this world"), Longshore believes that both creating and buying art is a very "intimate" experience. "I hope my zest for life radiates across all my paintings," she says, "and that this is something everyone can relate to."
