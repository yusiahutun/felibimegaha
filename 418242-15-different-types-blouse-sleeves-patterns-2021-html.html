15 different types of blouse sleeves and patterns in 2021

Sleeves are the focal point of any blouse or dress. They give the outfit a stylish finish depending on the design. However, today, sleeves are much more than long, short or sleeveless. The fashion industry has seen different types of blouse sleeves invented over the past few years, and more variations are still coming up.

Fashion enthusiasts are keen to try out different types of blouse sleeves for a wide range of attires. Some of these creations have been in existence for a while now and have stood the test of time. Others have been adopted recently and have quickly become the go-to option for formal and informal outfits.

15 different types of blouse sleeves in 2021

There are different types of sleeve designs for blouse or dress ensembles. Netizens keen to stay up-to-date with the latest trends in the industry have been searching for these creative designs.

The fashion industry is evolving every day, and designers have to keep up with the changes. Whatever your design of choice, sleeves should fit properly and be comfortable. Take a look at this compilation of different types of sleeves designs with names and pictures:

1. Cap sleeves

A cap sleeve is short in length and does not stretch far from the shoulder. Therefore, it only covers the top of the arm. This design of sleeves has no fabric under the arm, so it does not go below the armpit. Hence, it looks like a cap. The seams on the cap design can be elastic or loose.

Cap sleeves were first used as undergarments in America during World War 1. The design became popular in the 1990s. In India, these sleeves began to appear in lehengas and sarees in the last decade. Cap sleeves are best suited for blouses with sweetheart, scoop, or square necklines.

2. Full-length sleeves

Full sleeve designs work best for those who want to cover their arms fully. This design can either be sewn onto the bodice separately or as an extension of the blouse material. The fabric on the arms can be the same one used on the blouse. However, you can also go for a blouse with lace, net, or sheer fabric from the shoulder to the wrist.

If you wear a full sleeve blouse, one crucial fashion tip is to ensure that it fits your arms perfectly. Otherwise, it will look baggy and loose-fitting. Long sleeves are a perfect fashion piece when you want your blouse to stand out.

3. Three-quarter sleeves

Three-quarter sleeves can end anywhere between your elbow and wrist. Some fashion designers say that these blouses are designed to show off the wearer's fancy bracelet, bangle, or watch, so they are sometimes referred to as bracelet sleeves. This sleeves design pattern works best with printed blouses or saree blouse designs.

4. Butterfly sleeves

The butterfly design has its origin in the Philippines. As its name suggests, the sleeve flares out from the shoulder, but it does not cover the arm fully. It has a puff at the top and is loose near the hem. Butterfly sleeves are unique and elegant and do not have a cuff.

These sleeves are sometimes known as angel sleeves. The idea behind this design is that the puffed sleeve spreads out as you move your arm. Butterflies have a gentle drape and work best with medium or light fabrics.

5. Bell sleeves

The bell design of sleeves is also known as peasant sleeves. The bell is fitted around the shoulder and upper arm and flares out to the wrist like a bell. Blouses with this design are a bold fashion statement and are an excellent option if you want your outfit to stand out.

Bell sleeves have different variations, including the one-piece flared bell piece and the circular flounce bell sleeve. These types are dependent on the blouse design that you want to achieve.

6. Raglan sleeves

The raglan is also known as a peasant sleeve. It extends from the garment's neckline and not the shoulder, which allows better movement. The raglan design is sewn together at the neck and hem with a drawstring or elastic. These trendy arms are left loose for extra comfort and also look equally flattering.

7. Kimono sleeves

The Japanese attire, 'kimono', a long gown made of silk and decorated with floral patterns, inspired this design name. A kimono sleeve is sewn as one piece with the garment’s bodice and not separately.

Kimono sleeves are wide with a uniform circumference all around. Designers have created these sleeve patterns for dresses and blouses, and they are found mainly on Chinese-style robes.

8. Bishop sleeves

Bishop sleeves are stylish and feminine. The full sleeves have a fitted upper part and flared lower portion and are gathered tightly at the cuff. The design flares out from the shoulder, giving volume from the sleeve to the cuff.

The sleeves can be slightly full or flowy and drapery, depending on the fabric used. The design also comes in different variations depending on the attire to be created.

9. Off-shoulder sleeves

Off-shoulders are trendy and stylish, cutting across all age groups. They are popular with dresses, tops, and saree blouses and are suited for any social event. Off-shoulders draw attention to your collar bone and are the ultimate head-turner.

The sleeves lie on your upper arm, and you can customize their length according to your taste and preference. Designers recommend that you keep the pattern on the blouse simple and use fabrics like velvet, lace, and silk.

The off-shoulder has variations like the cut-out sleeve, which is also known as the cold shoulder sleeve. This design hangs on the bodice from the sides and not the top part, leaving a hole that exposes the shoulders.

10. Leg of mutton sleeves

The different types of sleeves are inspired by what they resemble. For example, the leg of the mutton sleeve is gathered and padded by the shoulder and upper arm and then fitted on the forearm, resembling a sheep's leg.

The sleeve is full in length and is tight from the elbow to the wrists. The puff at the top can vary in fullness depending on how pronounced the wearer wants the blouse to become. This design is ideal for party wear and outdoor social events, and also vintage attire.

11. Spaghetti strap sleeve

Spaghetti straps, also known as noodle straps, are glamorous, and they also make a bold fashion statement. This design provides minimal coverage on the shoulders.

You can wear a blouse with spaghetti strap sleeves with lightweight sarees. You can also rock the blouse together with a strapless blouse that needs more support.

12. Dolman sleeves

Dolman means a robe, and the sleeves are also known as Magyar sleeves. The Dolman design has a deep armhole, and the sleeve gets progressively narrower towards the wrist. The sleeve is one continuous extension of the bodice.

The Dolman is also called a batwing sleeve due to its similarity to bats' wings. Dolmans look excellent on women's blouses, sweaters, and dresses.

13. Puffed sleeves

The puffed sleeve was initially known as a gigot. It is gathered using bands or elastic ribbons at the shoulder and the seam but is full and “puffy” in the middle. The puff is a result of the gathers.

Puffs were a popular design back in the day before disappearing for a while. They are now back in fashion and are a preferred option for a wide range of age groups.

Puff sleeves can be joined at the armhole and fit at the sleeve. They can also fit at the armhole and gathered at the sleeve. Other variations include the drawstring puff sleeve, where a drawstring or a cuff joins the sleeve, with the gathered hem forming a sort of frill.

14. Ruffled sleeves

Ruffle blouse designs have veered off from the usual straight designs. The ruffles can either be near the hem, at the elbow, or near the wrist. The sleeves are a playful addition to your blouse or saree and go well with several outdoor outfits.

The ruffled loose sleeves design has gathers at the hem of the sleeve. They also come in different variations, which all create beautiful and unique designs for blouses and dresses.

15. Petal sleeves

Petals are also called lapped or tulip sleeves. They are sewn together at the top with the edges overlapping. The layered panels look like petals and make short or skimpy sleeves. Petal-sleeved blouses are a head-turner and add a feminine flair to the outfit.
